Now that software shapes every day of our lives, we must make doubly sure it's working for us, and not for them. In particular, we need top level interfaces—home screens, notification centers, app stores, newsfeeds, and browsers— that prioritize what we, the users, truly want out of life. We can no longer tolerate interfaces that maximize what's important for software and media companies--for instance, time spent scrolling, app downloads, or ad revenue--at the expense of our daily lives. But what would user-aligned interfaces look like, and how would we discover what's truly important for each user, so as to make them?

Will & Intent will be an affiliation of designers, philosophers, economists, and OS researchers who believe in solving these problems. We will give the public a taste of how it feels to use technology they don't have to fight with. Once the difference is recognized as a competitive advantage, we believe the industry will race to provide an OS that's good for people, not just good for eyeballs or sales.

Projects

  • The Collective Experience Project — a database of the values behind and the outcomes of user engagements with various URLs, includes a taxonomy of valueable things, which accounts for what people find important as they browse the web, use apps, and schedule thier lives, and a chrome extension called Hindsight — which matches your web use to your underlying values and goals
  • Doubt Club SF — a well-structured monthly event for leaders in the SF tech scene to start thinking philosophically about their impact and about media impact in general
  • Human Interface Guidelines for Expressive, Social, and Informed Choices — a new HIG for a new world; in-depth exploration of design patterns for allowing users to use their device to choose according to their own values
