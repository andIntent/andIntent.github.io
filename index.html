Software is no longer just for making or learning, now. Software is for living. Software is full-time.

We need OSes that are organized by what we, the users, truly want out of life, rather than what software and media companies can get out of us.

Will + Intent is a loose affiliation of designers, philosophers, economists, and operating systems researchers who believe this is possible, and that once the public has a taste of it, the industry will be forced to provide an OS that's good for people, not just good for eyeballs.

We're focused on delivering those tastes. We are in the business of software tastings. A new kind of advocacy.

We're starting with redesigns for top level interfaces—home screens, notification centers, app stores, and browsers—and with data collection efforts that focus on what's important to people, rather than what they can be tricked into buying or downloading.

Projects

  • The Collective Experience Project — a database of the values behind and the outcomes of user engagements with various URLs, includes a taxonomy of valueable things, which accounts for what people find important as they browse the web, use apps, and schedule thier lives, and a chrome extension called Hindsight — which matches your web use to your underlying values and goals
  • Doubt Club SF — a well-structured monthly event for leaders in the SF tech scene to start thinking philosophically about their impact and about media impact in general
  • Human Interface Guidelines for Expressive, Social, and Informed Choices — a new HIG for a new world; in-depth exploration of design patterns for allowing users to use their device to choose according to their own values
