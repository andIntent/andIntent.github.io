Now that software shapes every day of our lives, we must make doubly sure it's working for us, and not for them. In particular, we need top level interfaces (home screens, notification centers, app stores, newsfeeds, and browsers) that prioritize what we, the users, truly want out of life. We must no longer tolerate interfaces designed to maximize what's important for software and media companies—for instance, time spent scrolling, app downloads, or ad revenue—at the expense of our daily lives. But what would a notification screen organized by what's important to us look like? And if different things are truly important for different people, how can software come to support every kind of user?

Will & Intent will be an affiliation of designers, philosophers, economists, and OS researchers who believe in solving these problems. We want to show users how much better it is when technology doesn't have its own agenda. If we can communicate the appeal, we believe the industry will race to provide an OS that's good for people, and software that's just good for "engagement" or sales will be a thing of the past.

Projects

  • The Collective Experience Project — a database of the values behind and the outcomes of user engagements with various URLs, includes a taxonomy of valueable things, which accounts for what people find important as they browse the web, use apps, and schedule thier lives, and a chrome extension called Hindsight — which matches your web use to your underlying values and goals
  • Doubt Club SF — a well-structured monthly event for leaders in the SF tech scene to start thinking philosophically about their impact and about media impact in general
  • Human Interface Guidelines for Expressive, Social, and Informed Choices — a new HIG for a new world; in-depth exploration of design patterns for allowing users to use their device to choose according to their own values
